# HAD Architecture

## Overview

The HAD (Human Decides. AI Designs.) architecture is a cloud-assisted, AI-driven enterprise engineering platform designed to support the complete lifecycle of Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) systems.

Unlike traditional ERP development approaches, HAD separates engineering intelligence from enterprise-specific knowledge while ensuring that humans remain responsible for all critical business decisions.

---

# Architecture Layers

The HAD architecture consists of five logical layers:

1. Human Layer
2. Design Layer
3. Cloud Intelligence Layer
4. Local Runtime Layer
5. Enterprise System Layer

---

# Human Layer

The Human Layer represents business analysts, consultants, architects, and decision makers.

Humans define business goals, requirements, approvals, and organizational policies.

AI provides recommendations, but every critical decision requires human approval.

**Human Decides. AI Designs.**

---

# Design Layer

The Design Layer uses BPMN-AI as the primary visual design language.

Business processes become structured prompts that AI can understand and transform into enterprise system artifacts.

Generated artifacts may include:

* Forms
* Database Entities
* APIs
* Web Services
* Reports
* Dashboards
* Documentation

---

# Cloud Intelligence Layer

The Cloud Core acts as the engineering brain of the HAD platform.

Its responsibilities include:

* AI guidance
* Shared engineering intelligence
* Continuous collective learning
* Deployment planning
* Infrastructure recommendation
* AI support
* Partner ecosystem
* Model improvements

The Cloud Core never stores organization-specific confidential business data permanently.

---

# Local Runtime Layer

The Local Runtime executes inside the customer's infrastructure.

It contains:

* Enterprise database
* Business processes
* Forms
* APIs
* Documents
* Local AI memory
* Organization-specific knowledge

Sensitive business information remains under the organization's control.

---

# Enterprise System Layer

The generated ERP system operates inside the Local Runtime and provides all enterprise functionality.

Examples include:

* Human Resources
* Finance
* Procurement
* Inventory
* Logistics
* CRM
* Production

---

# Human Approval Model

Every important architectural decision follows the HAD philosophy:

AI proposes.

Human approves.

The platform never performs critical business changes without explicit human confirmation.

---

# Shared Intelligence

The Cloud Core continuously improves its engineering knowledge by learning from generalized implementation patterns.

Each organization benefits from improvements while maintaining privacy of its own enterprise knowledge.

This principle is summarized as:

**Shared Intelligence. Private Knowledge.**

---

# Living Documentation

Every architectural component automatically generates documentation.

Whenever a process, form, API, entity, or deployment configuration changes, the documentation is updated automatically, ensuring continuous synchronization between the system and its documentation.

---

# Architecture Philosophy

The architecture of HAD is guided by five principles:

* Human Decides. AI Designs.
* Shared Intelligence. Private Knowledge.
* Continuous Collective Learning.
* Design Once. Deploy Anywhere.
* Living Documentation.
