# HAD Architecture

## Overview

HAD (Human Decides. AI Designs.) is an AI-driven enterprise engineering architecture that enables organizations to analyze, design, deploy, and continuously improve Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) systems through collaboration between humans and Artificial Intelligence.

Unlike traditional ERP development approaches, HAD treats AI as an intelligent engineering assistant rather than an autonomous decision maker. Human experts remain responsible for defining business goals, approving architectural decisions, and validating generated artifacts.

---

# Architecture Vision

The primary objective of the HAD architecture is to separate global engineering intelligence from organization-specific knowledge while enabling continuous AI-assisted enterprise engineering.

The architecture follows four fundamental principles:

* Human Decides. AI Designs.
* Shared Intelligence. Private Knowledge.
* Continuous Collective Learning.
* Design Once. Deploy Anywhere.

---

# Architecture Layers

The HAD platform consists of five logical layers:

## 1. Human Layer

Business analysts, ERP consultants, architects, managers, and stakeholders define business objectives, approve AI recommendations, and maintain full governance over enterprise decisions.

---

## 2. BPMN-AI Design Layer

Business processes are modeled using BPMN-AI.

Instead of being simple workflow diagrams, BPMN-AI models act as structured visual prompts that AI can understand and transform into enterprise system artifacts.

Generated artifacts may include:

* Forms
* Database entities
* APIs
* Web Services
* Reports
* Dashboards
* Documentation
* Deployment plans

---

## 3. Cloud Core

The Cloud Core is the engineering intelligence of the HAD platform.

Its responsibilities include:

* AI guidance
* Collective engineering knowledge
* Deployment assistance
* Infrastructure recommendation
* AI technical support
* Partner ecosystem
* Continuous model improvement

The Cloud Core continuously evolves while remaining independent from organization-specific confidential information.

---

## 4. Local Runtime

The Local Runtime operates inside the customer's infrastructure.

It stores:

* Enterprise data
* Business rules
* Local AI memory
* ERP applications
* Private documents
* Organization-specific knowledge

Sensitive enterprise information always remains under the organization's control.

---

## 5. Enterprise System Layer

The generated ERP system executes inside the Local Runtime.

Depending on business requirements, AI may generate or configure modules including:

* Human Resources
* Finance
* Procurement
* Inventory
* CRM
* Production
* Logistics
* Asset Management

---

# AI Agent Platform

HAD employs specialized AI agents that cooperate throughout the enterprise engineering lifecycle.

Examples include:

* Design Agent
* Deployment Agent
* Documentation Agent
* Support Agent
* Advisor Agent

Each agent performs a specialized responsibility while collaborating with both the Cloud Core and the Local Runtime.

---

# Human Approval Model

AI may analyze, recommend, generate, optimize, and automate engineering tasks.

However, all strategic and business-critical decisions require explicit human approval.

This principle represents the core philosophy of HAD:

**Human Decides. AI Designs.**

---

# Living Documentation

Every enterprise artifact generated by HAD automatically produces documentation.

Whenever processes, forms, entities, APIs, reports, or deployment configurations change, the related documentation is automatically updated, ensuring continuous synchronization between implementation and documentation.

---

# Shared Intelligence

The Cloud Core continuously learns from generalized engineering patterns while preserving organizational privacy.

Organizations benefit from collective improvements without exposing confidential enterprise knowledge.

This principle is summarized as:

**Shared Intelligence. Private Knowledge.**

---

# Future Evolution

The HAD architecture is designed as a continuously evolving platform.

Future versions will extend the architecture through new AI agents, BPMN-AI components, deployment capabilities, enterprise integrations, and intelligent partner ecosystems while preserving human governance over all business decisions.
